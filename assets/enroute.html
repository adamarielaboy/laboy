In collaboration with: Hansel Huang

Methodologies

To be Fed or to be Fab

We combined our previous design research projects on food psychology and retail and found a common ground: The human pursuit of self-identity. In an age of constant exposure to social currency, people are moved by offline and online social experiences that nurture one’s identity. To many people, food and fashion are “identity vehicles”.

Travel: Unleashing Behaviors

Food and fashion are phenomenons that have moved from being physical and physiological necessities. Nowadays, they enable knowledge, identity, and empowerment.

It's a Fragmented Memory...

Traveling is planning, some do a lot, some prefer to just buy the tickets and let it “flow”. For curious travelers, the takeaway must be learning. If you’re spending money on the trip, might as well take the best out of it! But sometimes traveling is fragmented. From the moment we start researching, we open a million tabs, store the findings in notes, email, or an online shareable document. At the destination, we rely on a GPS and previous experiences. What are the takeaways after the trip is completed? Do we remember what we were looking for? What those memories look like in a week? A month? A year? It fades away… and we’d like to avoid that.

The Design Solution

We built our product having in mind the following archetype: an avant-garde, curious hedonist who travels not only to learn from another place, but to know what resonates with their ever-adaptable identity.
